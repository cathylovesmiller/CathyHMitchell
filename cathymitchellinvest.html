1. Victim Information

  • Name: Cathy Hem Mitchell
  • Date of Birth: June 6, 1979
  • Age: 23
  • Sex: Female
  • Occupation: Child Protective Services Case Worker
  • Home Address: 427 E. Magnolia Blvd., Apt. 3B, Emmet, ID
  • Missing Since: July 2, 2002 (Reported by coworker)

2. Location of Discovery

  • Date: July 28, 2002
  • 3:32 AM
  • Location: Wooded area behind Magnolia Apartments, 427 E. Magnolia Blvd., Emmet, ID
  • Caller: Charles Rendon (Dog walker, Apt. 1A)

3. Summary of Initial Response

Officers responded to the scene following a report of a body found in the wooded area behind the apartment complex. Upon arrival, Emmet County Police and Fire secured the scene. The victim was located 30 feet into the wooded area behind her residence, partially covered in foliage and dirt.

The body was severely decomposed. Emmet County Medical Examiner responded and declared the victim deceased at 3:45 AM. The victim was later confirmed to be Cathy Hem Mitchell through dental records.

4. Scene Observations

  • Body lying face-down, partially buried under brush and loose soil
  • Visible cranial trauma and fractured ribs
  • Victim’s Nokia 7650 smartphone recovered nearby in the dirt
  • No signs of drag marks—suggests victim was killed near or at the discovery site
  • No defensive wounds on scene surface; presumed removed by environmental factors

5. Evidence Collected

  • Victim’s cellphone (submitted for digital forensics)
  • Blood samples (forwarded to forensic lab)
  • Hair and fiber samples (forwarded to crime lab)
  • Soil and trace material beneath victim’s nails (SAK kit submitted)
  • During the early days of investigation (December 21, 2001 - February 3, 2002), investigators managed to tap Cathy's cellphone, thought information accquired through this is highly confidential.

6. Autopsy Summary (Condensed)

Conducted by Dr. Laura Enns on July 28, 2002. Findings include:

  • Advanced decomposition; estimated time of death: July 1–2, 2002
  • Cause of death: Blunt force trauma to the head
  • Manner of death: Homicide
  • Multiple rib fractures and defensive injuries noted
  • No evidence of sexual assault; toxicology pending

7. Digital Forensics

Victim’s Nokia 7650 recovered in close proximity to the body. Device was damaged but accessible.

  • Several deleted messages recovered between September 28 2001 – December 1 2001, involving threats and confrontational exchanges
  • Phone logs and SIM data forwarded to cyber unit

8. Persons of Interest

  • Eric D. Palmer – Ex-boyfriend; multiple domestic disturbance calls in 2001. Claims not to have seen Cathy since November 6, 2001. This was a lie, as Eric's phone was later tapped and messages sent and recieved about meeting up in early December were discovered between him and Cathy
  • Stacy Lin – Co-worker at CPS. Reported Cathy missing. Claims Cathy had received threats related to a custody case.
  • Brian West – Father of child in open CPS case. Known to have made aggressive statements in office on December 18. An alibi has since been provided for the week she went missing, but he is still being tracked for further questioning.

9. Investigative Actions (To Date)

  • Secured and processed crime scene (completed)
  • Canvassed apartment building – no direct witnesses.
  • Search warrant executed on Cathy’s apartment (July 29)
  • Interviewed immediate neighbors and colleagues (ongoing)
  • Awaiting full toxicology and DNA results from lab

10. Next Steps

  • Conduct in-depth forensic analysis of cellphone contents
  • Trace and interview unknown number from final text
  • Issue warrant for Brian West if unable to locate
  • Complete autopsy toxicology review upon lab return
