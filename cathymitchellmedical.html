Autopsy Report

Case Number: 2002-0728-HO-0312

Decedent Name: Cathy Hem Mitchell

Date of Birth: June 6, 1979

Age: 23

Sex: Female

Date of Autopsy: July 28, 2002

Performed By: Dr. Laura Enns, M.D., Chief Medical Examiner

Location: Emmet County Coroner’s Facility, Emmet, ID

I. External Examination

The body is that of a small-framed, adult Caucasian female measuring 5 feet 6 inches in height and weighing approximately 128 pounds in life. The body exhibits advanced decomposition, with skin slippage, bloating, extensive insect activity, and evidence of animal scavenging on the lower extremities. The face is largely unrecognizable due to postmortem degradation.

  • State of remains: Mummification of distal limbs, adipocere formation noted in torso and left thigh. Missing index finger and thumb on the left hand.
  • Insect colonization: Advanced maggot development suggests colonization shortly after death
  • Livor/Rigor mortis: Not assessable due to decomposition
  • Postmortem Interval: Approximately 25–27 days
  • Estimated time of death: Between July 1–3, 2002
  • Identifying features: Tattoo (bird on left ankle), dental records, DNA (pending)

II. Injuries (Traumatic Findings)

  • Head: Depressed skull fracture (left temporal), 3.5 cm diameter, high-velocity blunt force trauma
  • Torso: Multiple rib fractures (left ribs 5–7), consistent with repeated strikes by a heavy object
  • Extremities: Perimortem fractures in metacarpals, consistent with defensive wounds

III. Internal Examination

Due to decomposition, internal organs are in varying stages of liquefaction and collapse.

  • Brain: Liquefied, cranial cavity with discolored fluid
  • Heart/Lungs: Unrecognizable structures
  • Abdomen: Collapsed intestine; stomach contents unidentifiable
  • No foreign objects or projectiles found

IV. Toxicology

Standard blood toxicology was not possible due to decomposition.

Samples taken: Liver tissue, vitreous humor, hair

Status: Toxicology pending (Idaho State Toxicology Lab)

V. Sexual Assault Examination

Decomposition limited examination. No conclusive evidence of sexual assault.

  • No visible pelvic trauma
  • Sexual Assault Kit (SAK #2002-0728-SAE) completed and forwarded
  • No semen or foreign DNA identified (preliminary)

VI. Conclusions

Cause of Death: Blunt force trauma to the head

Manner of Death: Homicide

Contributing Conditions: Decomposition, environmental exposure, defensive injuries

Time of Death: Estimated between late evening of July 1 and early morning of July 2, 2002

Signed: Dr. Laura Enns, M.D.

Date Signed: July 28, 2002
